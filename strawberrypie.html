Strawberry Rhubarb Pie


Savor the taste of summer with our Strawberry Rhubarb Pie! A symphony of sweet strawberries and tart rhubarb, baked to golden perfection in a flaky
crust. It's sunshine in every slice!





Ingredients:


- 3 cups fresh rhubarb, sliced into 1/2-inch pieces
- 3 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and sliced
- 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
- 1/4 cup cornstarch
- 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice
- 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 cup unsalted butter, chilled and cubed
- 1/4 cup granulated sugar
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 to 1/2 cup ice water
- 1 tablespoon unsalted butter, cut into small pieces (for dotting on top)
- 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)
- Additional sugar (for sprinkling)

Step by steps


For the Pie Crust:

1. In a food processor, combine flour, sugar, and salt. Pulse to mix.
2. Add chilled and cubed butter. Pulse until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
3. Gradually add ice water, one tablespoon at a time, and pulse until the dough comes together.
4. Divide the dough in half, shape into discs, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.


For the Pie Filling:

1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
2. In a large bowl, combine sliced rhubarb, sliced strawberries, sugar, cornstarch, ground cinnamon, vanilla extract, and lemon juice. Toss until the fruit is well coated.


Assembly:

1. Roll out one disc of pie dough and place it in a 9-inch pie dish.
2. Pour the prepared strawberry-rhubarb filling into the pie crust.
3. Dot the filling with small pieces of unsalted butter.
4. Roll out the second disc of pie dough and place it over the filling. Trim and crimp the edges to seal.
5. Cut a few slits on the top crust to allow steam to escape.
6. Brush the top crust with the beaten egg and sprinkle with sugar.
7. Place the pie on a baking sheet (to catch any drips) and bake in the preheated oven for about 45-50 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.
8. Allow the pie to cool completely before serving.
