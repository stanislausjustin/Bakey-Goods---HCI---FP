Triple Chocolate cake


Immerse yourself in decadence with our Triple Chocolate Delight Cake. Three layers of pure chocolate bliss - moist chocolate cake,
velvety chocolate ganache, and a symphony of chocolate chips. Indulge in the ultimate chocolate lover's dream and make every occasion a celebration
of sweet extravagance!





Ingredients:


- 2 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 3/4 cups granulated sugar
- 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
- 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
- 1 1/2 teaspoons baking soda
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 2 large eggs
- 1 cup whole milk
- 1/2 cup vegetable oil
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
- 1 cup boiling water


For the Chocolate Ganache:

- 1 cup heavy cream
- 8 ounces semi-sweet or bittersweet chocolate, finely chopped

Step by steps


1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.

2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.

3. Add eggs, milk, vegetable oil, and vanilla extract. Beat on medium speed for about 2 minutes until the batter is well combined.

4. Gradually add the boiling water to the batter, mixing until smooth. The batter will be thin, but that's okay.

5. Divide the batter evenly among the prepared pans and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow the cakes to cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then transfer to wire racks to cool completely.



For the Chocolate Ganache:

1. In a small saucepan, heat the heavy cream over medium heat until it just begins to simmer. Remove from heat.

2. Add the finely chopped chocolate to the hot cream. Let it sit for a minute, then stir until smooth and glossy.

3. Allow the ganache to cool to room temperature or until it reaches a spreadable consistency.



Assembly:

1. Place one chocolate cake layer on a serving plate. Spread a layer of chocolate ganache on top.

2. Add the second cake layer and repeat with another layer of ganache.

3. Top with the third cake layer and frost the entire cake with the remaining ganache.

4. Allow the cake to set before slicing and serving.
